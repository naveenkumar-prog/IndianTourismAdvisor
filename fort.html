Facts about Indian Forts

Srirangapatna Fort Soak up the iconic tomb of Tipu Sultan

" Srirangapatna Fort is a heritage structure that formed the epicentre of Tipu Sultan's kingdom in Karnataka. It spans over 240 acres with four palatial gates and palaces that greet visitors with its sheer magnificence. Originally, the monument was built in the year 1454 and rebuilt numerous times by different rules who captured it. It is one of the most popular historical sites in Karnataka and lures art lovers and heritage buffs.

Amer Fort The Fort overlooks Maota Lake

Standing high up on a hill, the historic Amer Fort was built by the Meenas and later captured by Raja Man Singh I. It stands right next to Lake Maota, the principal source of water in Amer. The fort was named after the temple of Ambikeshwara that is nestled atop Cheel ka Teela. This palatial monument is a UNESCO World Heritage Site under Rajasthan Hill Forts.

Junagarh Fort A captivating assortment of temples and palaces built from red sandstones

Located in the city of Bikaner, Junagarh Fort is an ancient fortification built in the 20th century. The fort was built in the 15th century by Rao Bika who was also responsible for the development of Bikaner. However, the fort flourished during the reign of Raja Rai Singhji and he turned it into an architectural feat. The dazzling interiors with an enticing amalgamation of Rajput and western architecture allure countless visitors every year.
